**Anti-fake-done note (G2, cluster C-07).** The N-generality leaves (β, γ) must NOT close on a Rust unit test against a synthetically-constructed `Value::Tensor` alone — that is the rejected synthetic-input signal. Their binding completion requires the **`.ri`-source** path (α's `matrix(...)`/`diag(...)` constructors feeding `reify eval`), which is why α is a hard prerequisite and ζ is the integration gate. Building β/γ without α would reproduce the C-02/C-07 fake-done pattern (mechanism with no source-reachable consumer).

**Numeric-floor ledger (G6).** Conditioning is the only floor in play (rows 6, 10). All asserted signals use well-conditioned inputs (integer-determinant matrices, diagonal/orthogonal eigenproblems) where the `1e-9` tolerance sits comfortably above the `κ·ε` floor. No signal asserts machine-exact equality on a general dense computation.
